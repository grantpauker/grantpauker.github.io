Research

My research has focused on understanding biological control systems, specifically mapping the connection between a leechâ€™s neural circuitry and its locomotion.

By analyzing data from real swimming leeches, I identified valid parameters for a grey-box model that captures the interactions between the neural circuitry, body dynamics, and surrounding fluid dynamics.

With these parameters, I successfully closed the feedback loop of the model, enabling it to accurately simulate the leech's swimming behavior.

We are now conducting perturbation tests on the simulation to assess its robustness and accuracy.

This work is progressing toward publication, as we aim to further insights on how biological control systems can inspire more adaptive robotic controllers.
